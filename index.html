What changed on June 22, 2026 — and why the calculators we checked are now wrong

Etsy updated its regulatory operating fees for several countries effective June 22, 2026. These are percentage fees added on top of transaction fees for sellers in specific countries. Of six popular free calculators we tested on July 23, 2026, none applied the new rates — most never modeled these fees at all (the one accurate tool we found covers the UK only).

  • Regulatory operating fees — country-specific %, changed June 22, 2026
  • VAT/GST on seller fees — if you haven't given Etsy a VAT ID, most non-US sellers pay VAT on top of every Etsy fee
  • Offsite Ads on the whole order — the 12/15% is taken from the shipping you charge as well, not just the item price
  • Processing fees on tax-inclusive totals — you pay processing on sales tax Etsy collects and keeps

FAQ

Is this calculator accurate?

Every rate is taken from Etsy's official fee documentation and re-verified on the date shown above, with sources linked below. If Etsy changes fees, we update the data file — the logic itself is open in the page source. It's still an estimate: your exact bill can differ with promotions, refunds, or country tax specifics.

Do you see my numbers?

No. The calculator runs entirely in your browser. Nothing you type is sent anywhere — you can open this page, disconnect from the internet, and it keeps working.

Why does my margin drop so much with Offsite Ads?

Because the 12–15% is charged on the order total including shipping, on top of all the regular fees. On a low-priced item with paid shipping, an Offsite Ads order can cost you ~30% of your revenue in fees alone. That's the main thing sellers underestimate.
