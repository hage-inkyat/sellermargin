What changed on June 22, 2026 — and why the calculators we tested get non-US orders wrong

Etsy updated its regulatory operating fees for several countries effective June 22, 2026. We hand-tested 9 popular free calculators on July 23, 2026 with identical orders: none applied the new rates to a UK, French, or Canadian order. The two tools that advertise multi-country support understated a UK seller's fees by roughly 20–25% (one applies US processing rates worldwide; the other has a bug that effectively never charges the regulatory fee). None modeled VAT charged on seller fees. US-only tools were accurate — for US sellers.

  • Regulatory operating fees — country-specific %, changed June 22, 2026
  • VAT/GST on seller fees — if you haven't given Etsy a VAT ID, most non-US sellers pay VAT on top of every Etsy fee
  • Offsite Ads on the whole order — the 12/15% is taken from the shipping you charge as well, not just the item price
  • Processing fees on tax-inclusive totals — you pay processing on sales tax Etsy collects and keeps

FAQ

Is this calculator accurate?

Every rate is taken from Etsy's official fee documentation and re-verified on the date shown above, with sources linked below. If Etsy changes fees, we update the data file — the logic itself is open in the page source. It's still an estimate: your exact bill can differ with promotions, refunds, or country tax specifics.

Aren't there other free calculators?

Yes — and for US sellers several are accurate; we verified that ourselves. The gap is outside the US: of the 9 popular tools we hand-tested on July 23, 2026, none computed a correct UK, French, or Canadian order (stale regulatory rates, missing VAT on fees, US processing rates applied worldwide). If you're a US seller who only needs one order at a time, any accurate tool is fine — including this one.

How does the Canada estimate work?

Etsy publishes which fees are taxed for Canadian sellers (by registration status and whether Etsy collected buyer tax on the order) but not the numeric rates. We apply your province's GST/HST rate (plus BC PST or Quebec QST) to the fees Etsy says are taxable. It's clearly labeled an estimate; your monthly Etsy invoice is authoritative, and you can type its amount into the manual-override field instead.

Do you see my numbers?

No. The calculator runs entirely in your browser. Nothing you type is sent anywhere — you can open this page, disconnect from the internet, and it keeps working.

Why does my margin drop so much with Offsite Ads?

Because the 12–15% is charged on the order total including shipping, on top of all the regular fees. On a low-priced item with paid shipping, an Offsite Ads order can cost you ~30% of your revenue in fees alone. That's the main thing sellers underestimate.
